# Comparative Analysis: Senolytics vs NAD+ Boosters vs Epigenetic Reprogramming

**Date:** 2026-04-08  
**Focus:** Elderly (60-80) Rejuvenation

---

## Executive Summary

| Approach | Status (2026) | Safety for Elderly | Efficacy Evidence | Timeline to Market |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Senolytics** | Phase 1/2 trials | ✅ Established | ⚠️ Moderate | 3-5 years |
| **NAD+ Boosters** | Phase 2/3 trials | ✅ Established | ✅ Strong (18% VO2) | 2-4 years |
| **Epigenetic Reprogramming** | First Phase 1 started | ⚠️ Unknown (new) | ⚠️ Pre-clinical | 5-10 years |

**Best near-term for 60-80:** NAD+ boosters (proven safety + functional improvement)  
**Best long-term potential:** Epigenetic reprogramming (fundamentally reverses cellular age)

## Detailed Comparison

### 1. SENOLYTICS

**Mechanism:** Clear senescent "zombie" cells that accumulate with age and secrete inflammatory SASP (Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype)

**Key Drugs:**
- Dasatinib + Quercetin (DQ) — most studied
- Fisetin — natural senolytic
- Navitoclax (ABT-263)

**Elderly-Specific Evidence:**
- Lancet eBioMedicine (2025): Pilot study in older adults at risk for Alzheimer's — safety confirmed, cognitive/mobility effects being assessed
- Mayo Clinic: Senolytics decrease senescent cells in humans with diabetic kidney disease
- NIA (2025): Phase 2 trial in postmenopausal women — subtle impact on bone health

**Pros for Elderly:**
- ✅ Safety established in human trials
- ✅ Targets inflammation (key driver of age-related decline)
- ✅ Oral delivery possible
- ✅ Multiple companies racing

**Cons for Elderly:**
- ⚠️ Efficacy still moderate — clears cells but doesn't restore function
- ⚠️ Optimal dosing/regimen still being refined
- ⚠️ Must clear periodically (cells re-accumulate)

**Timeline:** First approved senolytic ~2029-2030 (if trials succeed)

---

### 2. NAD+ BOOSTERS (NMN, NR)

**Mechanism:** Boost NAD+ levels which decline ~50% by age 50, affecting sirtuins, PARPs, CD38/CD157 enzymes, and cellular metabolism

**Key Compounds:**
- NMN (β-nicotinamide mononucleotide) — direct precursor
- NR (nicotinamide riboside) — precursor
- NRPT (Nicotinamide riboside and pterostilbene)

**Elderly-Specific Evidence:**
- **Harvard 2026:** First large-scale NMN trial (N=240, adults 60+) showed **18% improvement in VO2 max** — significant functional benefit
- Nature Metabolism (2026): Direct comparison of 3 NAD+ precursors — NR raised NAD+ 2x more than NMN
- Multiple trials show safety and metabolic benefits

**Pros for Elderly:**
- ✅ Strong efficacy data (VO2 max, metabolic markers)
- ✅ Excellent safety profile
- ✅ Already available as supplements (though quality varies)
- ✅ Oral delivery
- ✅ Works systemically

**Cons for Elderly:**
- ⚠️ Must take continuously
- ⚠️ Not a true "rejuvenation" — maintains rather than reverses
- ⚠️ Results vary by individual

**Timeline:** Already in advanced trials, FDA-approved therapeutic possible ~2028-2030

---

### 3. EPIGENETIC REPROGRAMMING (Yamanaka Factors)

**Mechanism:** Use OSKM (Oct4, Sox2, Klf4, c-Myc) to reset epigenetic marks to youthful state. Partial reprogramming (cyclical/partial) aims to avoid tumor risk.

**Current Status:**
- **Feb 2026:** FDA cleared Life Biosciences ER-100 — first human trial
- Delivery: AAV2 gene therapy to eye (optic neuropathies)
- Uses **3 Yamanaka factors** (not all 4) — designed to be safer

**Elderly-Specific Evidence:**
- All evidence currently pre-clinical (mice, primates)
- Showed reversal of epigenetic age in mice
- Restored vision in primate models

**Pros for Elderly:**
- ⭐ Potential to fundamentally reverse cellular age
- ⭐ Could address root cause of aging, not just symptoms
- ⭐ Inducible system (can turn on/off)

**Cons for Elderly:**
- ❌ **Unknown safety in humans** — first trials just started
- ⚠️ Current delivery is eye-specific only — systemic delivery unsolved
- ⚠️ Tumor risk (though reduced with partial reprogramming)
- ⚠️ Long-term effects unknown

**Timeline:** First approved reprogramming therapy ~2031-2035 (if trials succeed)

## Systemic Delivery Comparison

| Approach | Systemic Delivery Status |
|----------|-------------------------|
| Senolytics | ✅ Oral pills work systemically |
| NAD+ Boosters | ✅ Oral supplements work systemically |
| Reprogramming | ❌ Eye trials only; systemic unknown |

**For elderly systemic rejuvenation:** Senolytics and NAD+ boosters currently have the advantage

---

## Recommendation for Elderly (60-80)

### Near-term (now — 2028):
**Use NAD+ boosters (NMN or NR)** — proven safety, functional improvements (18% VO2), available now

### Medium-term (2028-2032):
**Combine NAD+ boosters + senolytics** — complementary mechanisms (metabolism + inflammation)

### Long-term (2032+):
**If reprogramming trials succeed:** Could become gold standard for fundamental rejuvenation
