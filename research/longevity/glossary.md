# Longevity Research Glossary

**Purpose:** Make this research accessible to everyone, regardless of background

---

## A

### Aging (Biological)
The progressive decline in cellular and tissue function over time. Different from chronological age (how old you are) — biological age can be younger or older than your actual age.

### Autophagy
The process where cells "clean out" damaged components. Think of it as cellular recycling. Declines with age; boosting it may extend lifespan.

---

## C

### Cellular Senescence
When cells stop dividing and enter a "zombie" state. They don't die but secrete inflammatory factors that damage neighboring cells. A key target of senolytics.

### Chronological Age vs Biological Age
- **Chronological age:** How many years you've lived
- **Biological age:** How old your cells/tissues appear based on markers

You can have a biological age lower or higher than your chronological age.

### Clinical Trial Phases
- **Phase 1:** Safety testing (small group)
- **Phase 2:** Efficacy testing (larger group)
- **Phase 3:** Large-scale verification
- **FDA approval:** Can be prescribed

---

## D

### DNA
The genetic code that tells your cells how to function. Contains instructions for making proteins and regulating cellular behavior.

---

## E

### Epigenetic Reprogramming
Resetting the "software" of cells (how genes are expressed) without changing the "hardware" (DNA sequence). Uses Yamanaka factors to reverse cellular age.

### Epigenetics
The study of how behavior and environment can cause changes that affect the way your genes work. Unlike DNA sequence, epigenetic marks can change throughout life.

---

## G

### Gene Therapy
A treatment that delivers genetic material into cells to treat or prevent disease. Can add new genes, modify existing ones, or turn genes on/off.

### GDF11 (Growth Differentiation Factor 11)
A protein found in young blood that has rejuvenating effects on old tissues. One of the specific factors identified in young blood research.

---

## I

### IGF-1 (Insulin-like Growth Factor 1)
A hormone/growth factor that plays important roles in growth and development. Levels decrease with age; involved in young blood's rejuvenating effects.

### Inflammatory Markers
Substances in the blood that indicate inflammation. High levels are associated with aging and age-related diseases. Examples: CRP, IL-6.

---

## L

### Lifespan vs Healthspan
- **Lifespan:** How long you live
- **Healthspan:** How long you live in good health, without chronic disease

The goal of longevity research is to extend both, but especially healthspan.

---

## M

### mTOR (mammalian Target of Rapamycin)
A protein pathway that regulates cell growth and metabolism. Inhibiting it (with drugs like rapamycin) can extend lifespan in animals.

---

## N

### NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide)
A coenzyme found in all living cells that's essential for metabolism and cellular energy. Levels decline ~50% by age 50, affecting many cellular processes.

### NMN (β-Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)
A molecule that serves as a direct precursor to NAD+. When taken as a supplement, it can boost NAD+ levels. One of the most researched anti-aging supplements.

### NR (Nicotinamide Riboside)
Another NAD+ precursor, similar to NMN. Found in small amounts in some foods. Some studies suggest it may raise NAD+ even higher than NMN.

---

## O

### OSKM (Yamanaka Factors)
Four transcription factors (Oct4, Sox2, Klf4, c-Myc) that can reprogram adult cells back to an embryonic-like state. Named after Shinya Yamanaka who discovered them (Nobel Prize 2012).

---

## P

### Parabiosis
The surgical joining of two organisms' circulatory systems. In aging research, connecting a young mouse to an old mouse shows rejuvenating effects in the old mouse.

### Plasma
The liquid component of blood that carries cells, hormones, and nutrients. Young plasma contains factors that may have rejuvenating properties.

---

## R

### Rapamycin
A drug that inhibits mTOR pathway. Extends lifespan in mice and is used in transplant medicine. Being studied for longevity effects in humans.

---

## S

### SASP (Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype)
The mixture of inflammatory signals, growth factors, and enzymes that senescent cells secrete. Causes "bystander effect" — damaging nearby healthy cells.

### Senescence
See "Cellular Senescence"

### Senolytics
Drugs that selectively eliminate senescent ("zombie") cells. The goal is to clear these problem cells to reduce inflammation and improve tissue function.

**Common senolytics:**
- Dasatinib + Quercetin (DQ)
- Fisetin
- Navitoclax (ABT-263)

### Sirtuins
A family of proteins involved in cellular regulation, including metabolism, stress resistance, and aging. Require NAD+ to function — one reason NAD+ boosting may help.

---

## T

### Telomeres
Protective caps on the ends of chromosomes, like the plastic tips on shoelaces. They shorten with each cell division and are considered a "biological clock."

### Telomerase
An enzyme that can add length to telomeres. Active in stem cells and cancer cells; normally low in most adult cells.

---

## V

### VO2 Max
A measure of the maximum amount of oxygen a person can use during intense exercise. A key indicator of cardiovascular fitness and overall health. The Harvard NMN study showed 18% improvement in VO2 max.

---

## Y

### Yamanaka Factors
See "OSKM"

---

## Quick Reference: Approaches

| Term | What It Means | Goal |
|------|---------------|------|
| **NAD+ Boosters** | Raise NAD+ levels | Improve cellular metabolism |
| **Senolytics** | Clear zombie cells | Reduce inflammation |
| **Epigenetic Reprogramming** | Reset cellular age | Reverse aging |
| **Telomere Extension** | Lengthen chromosome caps | Reset cellular clock |
| **Young Blood Factors** | Use young plasma factors | Rejuvenate tissues |
| **Gene Therapy** | Modify genetic instructions | Treat root causes |

---

## Key Numbers to Know

- **NAD+ decline:** ~50% by age 50
- **Telomere shortening:** ~30-40 base pairs per year
- **VO2 max decline:** ~10% per decade after 30
- **Clinical trial duration:** Typically 5-10 years
