# Human Life Extension & Rejuvenation Research

**Last Updated:** 2026-04-08  
**Focus:** Extending human lifespan and helping elderly rejuvenate

---

## Executive Summary (as of April 2026)

A historic inflection point: **epigenetic reprogramming has entered human clinical trials**. For decades, the idea that aging could be actively reversed existed only in animal studies. In 2026, that boundary has dissolved. Multiple approaches are now in various trial stages.

---

## Major Approaches

### 1. Cellular Reprogramming / Epigenetic Reprogramming ⭐ MOST PROMISING

**What:** Uses OSKM (Yamanaka factors: Oct4, Sox2, Klf4, c-Myc) to reset epigenetic age. Partial reprogramming (cyclical expression) aims to reverse aging without creating tumors.

**Recent Developments:**
- **Feb 2026:** FDA cleared first human cellular reprogramming trial (Life Biosciences, ER-100) for optic neuropathies — first therapy directed at reversing diseases of aging by cellular rejuvenation
- March 2026: More companies entering the space
- Eye diseases chosen as first target because delivery is straightforward and measurable

**Companies:** Life Biosciences, Retro Biosciences, CytoGen, Shift Bioscience

**Status:** Human trials started 2026

---

### 2. Senolytics (Clearing Senescent Cells)

**What:** Drugs that selectively eliminate senescent ("zombie") cells that accumulate with age and cause inflammation (senescence-associated secretory phenotype/SASP).

**Key Drugs:** Dasatinib + Quercetin (DQ), Fisetin, Navitoclax

**Recent Developments:**
- Multiple Phase 1/2 trials completed and ongoing
- Lancet eBioMedicine (2025): Pilot study of senolytics for cognitive/mobility in older adults at risk for Alzheimer's
- Mayo Clinic: Trial shows senolytics decrease senescent cells in humans with diabetic kidney disease
- NIA (2025): Senolytic therapy shows subtle impact on age-related bone health

**Status:** Human trials active; safety established, efficacy being refined

---

### 3. NAD+ Boosters (NMN, NR)

**What:** Boost NAD+ levels which decline with age, affecting sirtuins, PARPs, and cellular metabolism.

**Recent Developments:**
- **Harvard 2026:** First large-scale NMN trial (N=240) showed **18% improvement in VO2 max** for adults 60+ (Cell Metabolism)
- Multiple trials show safety
- Ongoing debate on optimal dosing and formulation

**Status:** Human trials show promise; commercial supplements widely available but regulation varies

---

### 4. Telomere Extension

**What:** Lengthen telomeres (protective caps on chromosomes) which shorten with each cell division.

**Approaches:** Telomerase activators, gene therapy

**Status:** Early stage; risks of cancer (uncontrolled cell division) being studied

---

### 5. Young Blood Factors / Parabiosis

**What:** Studies showed young blood can rejuvenate old tissues. Focus on identifying specific factors (e.g., GDF11, CCL3).

**Status:** Research ongoing; controversial but active

---

### 6. Stem Cell Therapy

**What:** Replace aged stem cells or boost endogenous stem cell activity.

**Recent Developments:**
- Forever Labs and others pursuing autologous stem cell banking/transplantation
- Focus on mesenchymal stem cells for immune modulation

**Status:** Clinical trials ongoing

---

### 7. mTOR Inhibition (Rapamycin)

**What:** Rapamycin extends lifespan in mice; affects autophagy and protein synthesis.

**Status:** Used in transplant medicine; longevity trials ongoing

## For Elderly Rejuvenation Specifically

Most promising near-term interventions for elderly:

1. **NAD+ boosters** — Already showing functional improvements (VO2 max), proven safety, available now
2. **Senolytics** — Already in human trials, targeting age-related inflammation
3. **Epigenetic reprogramming** — 2026 trials will establish safety; efficacy data 2-3 years out

**Realistic expectation:** Combination approaches likely needed. First approved therapies probably 5-10 years away.

### Quick Recommendation for 60-80 age group

| Timeframe | Approach | Why |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| Now | NAD+ boosters (NMN/NR) | 18% VO2 improvement, proven safety |
| 2028+ | NAD+ + senolytics combo | Complementary mechanisms |
| 2032+ | If reprogramming succeeds | Fundamental age reversal |
