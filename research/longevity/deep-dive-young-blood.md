# Deep Dive: Young Blood / Plasma Factors

**Date:** 2026-04-08  
**Focus:** Specific factors identified, clinical potential, and rejuvenation mechanisms

---

## Executive Summary

Young blood plasma contains factors that can rejuvenate old tissues. After decades of parabiosis experiments, scientists are finally identifying specific proteins and pathways. Feb 2026 review paper highlights clinical translation progress.

---

## The Science

### Heterochronic Parabiosis
- Surgical joining of young and old mice circulatory systems
- Old mice show rejuvenation: improved muscle, brain, heart function
- Young mice show aging in some organs

### Key Findings Over Time

| Year | Discovery |
|------|-----------|
| 2005 | Young blood improves old mouse cognition (Conboy et al.) |
| 2014 | GDF11 identified as key factor for muscle rejuvenation |
| 2020 | CCL3 identified as brain rejuvenation factor |
| 2026 | IGF-1/IGF-1R signaling for neurovascular coupling |

---

## Specific Factors Identified (2026)

### 1. GDF11 (Growth Differentiation Factor 11)
- **What:** Myostatin-related protein
- **Effect:** Rejuvenates muscle, heart, brain
- **Evidence:** Injecting old mice with GDF11 improves function
- **Status:** Research stage, dosing being refined

### 2. CCL3 (Chemokine C-C motif ligand 3)
- **What:** Inflammatory chemokine
- **Effect:** Young plasma rejuvenates old brain via CCL3
- **Evidence:** 2020 study identified as key brain factor
- **Status:** Research stage

### 3. IGF-1 (Insulin-like Growth Factor 1)
- **What:** Growth factor, mediates many young blood effects
- **Effect:** Endothelial IGF-1/IGF-1R signaling crucial for neurovascular coupling
- **Evidence:** Feb 2026 study in GeroScience
- **Status:** Being studied as pathway

### 4. Other Factors
- **TIMP1:** Matrix metalloproteinase inhibitor, improves brain
- **Serum:** Young serum improves various tissues
- **Extracellular vesicles:** New research on messaging

## February 2026: Clinical Translation Review

**Paper:** "Plasma-based strategies for systemic rejuvenation: critical perspectives on clinical translation" (GeroScience, Feb 2026)

### Key Points:
1. **Specific factors being identified** — Moving beyond "young blood works" to "these proteins work"
2. **Multiple pathways** — No single "rejuvenation factor" — combination likely needed
3. **Clinical trials starting** — Therapeutic plasma exchange (TPE) being tested
4. **Safety concerns** — Immune reactions, unknown long-term effects

---

## Clinical Approaches

### 1. Young Plasma Transfusion
- **What:** Transfuse young plasma to elderly
- **Status:** Some clinics offer, but evidence limited
- **Risks:** Immune reactions, infections

### 2. Therapeutic Plasma Exchange (TPE)
- **What:** Replace patient's plasma with donor plasma
- **Evidence:** 2025 study showed biomarkers of biological age improvement
- **Status:** Clinical trials ongoing

### 3. Isolated Factors (Future)
- **What:** Give specific identified factors (GDF11, CCL3, etc.)
- **Status:** Pre-clinical / early clinical
- **Advantage:** More targeted, fewer side effects

## For Elderly Rejuvenation
